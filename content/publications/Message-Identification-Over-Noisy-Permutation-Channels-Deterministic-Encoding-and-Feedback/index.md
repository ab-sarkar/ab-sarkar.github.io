abstract: |
  We study message identification over the $q$-ary noisy permutation channel. The channel first permutes the symbols of the input vector by a randomly chosen permutation, and the symbols then pass through a discrete memoryless channel (DMC) with transition probability matrix $U$. It is known from earlier works that the message size for reliable communication over this channel can grow as $n^{R}$, where $R>0$ is defined as the rate. The capacity, defined as the supremum of achievable rates $R$, is proved to be $\frac{r-1}{2}$ when $U$ has strictly positive entries in the transition probability matrix, where $r$ is the rank of $U$. In this work, we study message identification over the noisy permutation channels under deterministic encoders, and also under noiseless feedback. We show that message size growing as $\epsilon_n n^{(r-1)/2}$ is identifiable under deterministic encoding for any $\epsilon_n \to 0$. We also prove weak and strong converse results under deterministic encoding for $U$ with strictly positive entries. The message sizes in the converse results differ from the achievability by a power of a logarithmic factor. Under blockwise noiseless feedback, we show that  $2^{2^{R(l-1)n}}$ messages are identifiable over $l$ blocks for $R < \hmax$, where $H_{\text{max}}$ denotes the maximum output entropy of $U$ over all input distributions. Furthermore, we prove a strong converse for message sizes $2^{2^{nlR}}$ if $R > \hmax$.
